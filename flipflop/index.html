Flip-flopping the color?

Late last week, the optical illusion has launched back into the mainstream, when a Twitter user posted a picture of flip-flops that could be interpreted on the same spectrum.

How does it work?

The New York Times published a piece analyzing how the dress illusion worked last year. The same principles apply here: essentially, the color can change depending on if you interpret the article of clothing to be in a blue-ish shadow or a yellow light. Your mind interprets the image and takes into account the lighting conditions, resulting in a different perceptions of color.
